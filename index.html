Cas Blaauw

Studying MSc Bioinformatics @ UCPH · Bioinformatics Student Assistant @ Clinical Microbiomics · Research Assistant @ Copenhagen Center for Glycomics

I'm a bioinformatician, currently studying the MSc Bioinformatics at the University of Copenhagen, and working as a part-time bioinformatician at Clinical Microbiomics on the side. Besides this, I am a research assistant working with Hiren Joshi and Ole Winther and the president of CBioVikings, the Danish regional student group of the ISCB. My main interest is how systems come together to form a greater whole, ranging from subway systems to online communities, but primarily understanding how the molecular mechanisms in a cell lead to sentient life. This originally drove me to study Molecular Life Sciences at Utrecht University, where I quickly realised that the large-scale understanding I sought requires bioinformatics. Three years of learning about programming and data analysis later, with a detour to the University of Hong Kong to study public policy, international relations and modern history, I'm now living in the European mecca for life sciences: Copenhagen. Learn more about my background on my LinkedIn, explore my code on GitHub, or read on for more details about my research and motivation.
